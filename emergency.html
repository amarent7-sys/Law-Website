Understanding Police Authorities: FIR vs. NCR

It is critical to distinguish between cognizable and non-cognizable reports. Police often exceed their legal limits; this comparative grid explains their powers.

First Information Report (FIR)

Applies strictly to Cognizable Offenses (e.g. money laundering, major financial fraud, unauthorized data breaches).

  • No Warrant Needed: Police can investigate without waiting for a Magistrate's authorization.
  • Arrest Power: Investigators can make immediate custodial arrests of suspects.
  • Legal Consequence: Direct court presentation and trial scheduling flow immediately.

Non-Cognizable Report (NCR)

Applies strictly to Non-Cognizable Offenses (e.g. petty disputes, defamation, minor commercial defaults).

  • Warrant Required: Police are legally prohibited from investigating without a Magistrate Order.
  • Zero Arrest Power: Police have no power to arrest without a specific judicially signed warrant.
  • Filing Method: Information is registered in the station diary and referred directly to the Magistrate.
